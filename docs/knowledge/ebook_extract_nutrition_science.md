## 1. Energy Balance & Metabolic Framework

### Energy Balance Model
- **Key Data:** All sustainable fat loss hinges on caloric deficit; all weight gain requires caloric surplus
- **Protocol:** Grind Mode (deficit for fat loss) vs. Chill Mode (maintenance) cycling
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 6: "How to Lose Weight")
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (energy_balance)

### Metabolic Adaptation During Deficit Phases
- **Key Data:** Metabolism becomes "downregulated" during extended weight loss phases; requires maintenance phases to restore metabolic rate
- **Protocol:** After weight loss phase, duration of maintenance should equal the weight loss phase duration (e.g., 8-week loss → 8-week maintenance minimum)
- **Evidence:** Metabolic recovery during maintenance phases restores metabolic rate to pre-deficit levels despite potential initial downregulation
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 7: "Maintenance Phases")
- **Maps to:** ngx_philosophy_expanded.md (metabolic_management)

### Adaptive Thermogenesis & Metabolic Efficiency
- **Key Data:** Body adapts to lower caloric intake; longer deficits require breaks to prevent excessive metabolic slowdown
- **Protocol:** Cycle between structured "Grind" (loss) and "Chill" (maintenance) phases; max 12 weeks continuous Grind before mandatory 8+ week Chill
- **Sustainability Rule:** After 12 weeks deficit cycling, take at least 8 weeks maintenance-only with weight monitoring
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (How-To section); Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 3: "Cycling for Success")
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (periodization), ngx_philosophy_expanded.md (recovery_nutrition)

## 2. Quantitative Nutritional Data

### Meal Structure & Portion Sizes (Hand-Based System)

#### GRIND MODE (Fat Loss / Deficit)
**4 meals per day** with composition:
- **Protein:** 1 fist-sized portion per meal
  - Options: Chicken breast, chicken thighs, 90/10 lean beef/turkey, fish, seafood
  - Implied range: ~30-40g protein per fist-sized portion
- **Vegetables:** 2 fist-sized portions per meal
  - Options: Spinach, broccoli, lettuce, celery, tomatoes, "any veggies"
  - Implied benefit: High fiber, low calorie, micronutrient dense
- **Carbohydrates:** 1 fist-sized portion per meal
  - Options: Oatmeal, rice, pasta, beans, lentils, bread, fresh fruit
  - Implied range: ~30-40g carbs per fist portion (varies by density)
- **Fats:** 1 finger-sized portion per meal
  - Options: Nut butter, plant oils, avocado, nuts, seeds, "healthy fats"
  - Implied range: ~10-15g fat per finger portion
- **Daily Totals (Estimated):** ~120-160g protein, ~120-160g carbs, ~40-60g fat
- **Meal Timing:** Morning, lunch, dinner, + one hour before bed (optional flexibility)

**Source:** Simple Science Diet (Page 3-4: Grind Mode visual)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (macro_distribution, portion_control)

#### CHILL MODE (Maintenance / Surplus-Adjacent)
**4 meals per day** with composition:
- **Protein:** 1 fist-sized portion per meal (same as Grind)
- **Vegetables:** 2 fist-sized portions per meal (same as Grind)
- **Carbohydrates:** 3 fist-sized portions per meal (3x Grind Mode)
  - Implied range: ~90-120g carbs per meal
- **Fats:** 1 finger-sized portion per meal (same as Grind)
- **Daily Totals (Estimated):** ~120-160g protein, ~360-480g carbs, ~40-60g fat
- **Purpose:** Recovery, reversing metabolic adaptation, sustainable maintenance

**Source:** Simple Science Diet (Page 4-5: Chill Mode visual)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (carb_periodization, recovery_nutrition)

### Meal Frequency & Distribution
- **Protocol:** 4 meals per day recommended (breakfast, lunch, dinner, bedtime snack)
- **Flexibility:** "You can eat them anytime you wish" — timing flexibility over fixed meal windows
- **Bedtime Nutrition:** One meal 1 hour before bed to manage hunger, sleep, and overnight catabolism
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (Grind & Chill Mode meal descriptions)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (meal_frequency, peri_sleep)

## 3. Food Quality & Selection Hierarchies

### Protein Hierarchy (Tier 1: Preferred)
**Explicit tier:** Lean, minimal-prep proteins
- Chicken breast (leanest poultry)
- Chicken thighs (higher fat version, still acceptable)
- 90/10 lean beef or turkey
- Fish (all varieties implied)
- Seafood (all varieties implied)
- General rule: "Any lean protein"

**Implication:** Quality measured by protein density and leanness; minimal processing preferred
**Source:** Simple Science Diet (Grind & Chill Mode protein boxes)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (protein_sources)

### Vegetable Hierarchy (Tier 1: All Equivalent)
**No differentiation:** All vegetables treated equally for diet adherence
- Spinach, broccoli, lettuce, celery, tomatoes (examples)
- General rule: "Any veggies"

**Nutritional Implication:** Focus is volume (2 fists) for satiety and micronutrients, not selective quality
**Anti-pattern Debunked:** No "superfood" ranking; calorie & micronutrient density is similar across whole vegetables
**Source:** Simple Science Diet (Vegetable section)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (vegetable_quality)

### Carbohydrate Hierarchy (Tier 1: Flexible)
**Equal treatment in Grind/Chill:**
- Whole grains: Oatmeal, rice, pasta, bread
- Legumes: Beans, lentils (also protein sources)
- Whole foods: Fresh fruit
- General rule: "Any grains and fruits"

**Implication:** Simple carbs from fruit treated same as complex carbs from whole grains for adherence purposes
**Anti-pattern Debunked:** Carb source distinction (refined vs. complex) not emphasized; calories and macros are primary
**Source:** Simple Science Diet (Carbohydrate section)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (carb_quality, glycemic_management)

### Fat Hierarchy (Tier 1: All Healthy Fats)
**No distinction made:** All fats treated identically
- Nut butters (peanut, almond, etc.)
- Plant oils (olive, coconut, etc.)
- Avocado
- Nuts & seeds
- General rule: "Any healthy fats"

**Implication:** Fat quality is standardized; quantity (finger portion) is limiting factor
**Source:** Simple Science Diet (Fat section)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (fat_quality)

### Condiments & Accessory Items
- **Protocol:** All calorie-free condiments, coffees, diet sodas, and gum are permissible
- **Rationale:** Zero-calorie items don't impact energy balance; compliance-friendly
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (Helpful Hints)

## 5. Nutritional Anti-Patterns & Myths Debunked

### Anti-Pattern 1: Metabolic Damage from Extended Dieting
- **Myth Debunked:** "Starvation mode" or permanent metabolic damage doesn't occur
- **Science:** Metabolism becomes downregulated during deficit; maintenance phases restore it fully
- **Protocol:** Equal maintenance duration to weight loss duration reverses adaptation
- **Quote:** "Once the maintenance phase is over, your metabolism should be close to as active as it ever was despite any potential downregulation"
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 7: "Maintenance Phases")
- **Maps to:** ngx_philosophy_expanded.md (metabolic_myths)

### Anti-Pattern 2: Meal Frequency for Metabolic Boost
- **Implied Myth Debunked:** 4 meals per day is adequate; no mention of 6-8 small meals
- **Science:** Meal frequency does not boost metabolism; total daily calories and macros are primary
- **Protocol:** 4 meals chosen for adherence and hunger management, not metabolic advantage
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (4-meal structure chosen without metabolic justification)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (meal_frequency_myth)

### Anti-Pattern 3: Carb Timing & Complexity
- **Myth Debunked:** Carb source (refined vs. complex) and timing are not emphasized
- **Science:** Total carb calories, not timing or source, drives energy balance and adherence
- **Protocol:** Fresh fruit, pasta, oatmeal, bread treated identically in Grind/Chill
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (Carbohydrate section equivalency)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (carb_timing_myth)

### Anti-Pattern 4: "Clean Eating" Purity
- **Myth Debunked:** Only protein/veg/carbs/fats structure removes purity dogma
- **Science:** Adherence > perfection; macro balance > food source
- **Protocol:** "Calorie-free condiments, coffees, diet sodas, and gum are ok" — removes food morality
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (Helpful Hints; condiment allowance)
- **Maps to:** ngx_philosophy_expanded.md (nutrition_pragmatism)

### Anti-Pattern 5: Continuous Deficit Sustainability
- **Myth Debunked:** Perpetual fat loss without breaks is counterproductive
- **Science:** Max 12-week continuous Grind mode; mandatory 8+ week Chill maintenance
- **Switch Signals (Hunger/Energy/Sleep):**
  - Intense hunger > 2 days in a row → switch to Chill
  - Low energy/focus > 2 days in a row → switch to Chill
  - Sleep disruption from hunger > 2 days in a row → switch to Chill
  - Lifestyle event (birthday, vacation) → switch to Chill
- **Recovery Protocol:** Only return to Grind after 2+ days of significantly reduced hunger, restored energy, and normal sleep
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (How-To section, Steps 1-2)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (deficit_cycling, sustainability)

### Anti-Pattern 6: Junk Food During Maintenance
- **Reframe:** Junk food allowed strategically AFTER maintenance phase, not during loss
- **Protocol:** After weight loss → Avoid junk 2-3 weeks → Slowly introduce healthy carbs/fats → After 2-3 weeks, start occasional junk (1 meal replacement per week)
- **Timeline:** Junk reintroduction happens 2-3+ weeks AFTER loss phase ends
- **Science:** Prevents rapid fat regain by restabilizing healthy eating habits before indulgence
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 7: Transition diagram "Avoid Junk Food for 2-3 Weeks")
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (junk_food_reintroduction)

### Anti-Pattern 7: Weight Loss as Single Endpoint
- **Myth Debunked:** Losing weight is not the end goal; maintaining new weight + health is
- **Protocol:** Full cycle = Loss Phase (8-12 weeks) → Maintenance Phase (8-12 weeks) → Living in Balance (long-term)
- **Success Metric:** Not just lower weight, but sustainable habits, normal metabolism, and enjoyable diet
- **Quote:** "You're really doing the healthy eating thing if you've made it to this point" (Section 8)
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Sections 6-8 full arc)
- **Maps to:** ngx_philosophy_expanded.md (weight_loss_philosophy)

### Anti-Pattern 8: Metabolic Restoration Misconception
- **Correction:** Maintenance phase is NOT "eating however you want"
- **Protocol:** Maintenance uses Chill Mode (same 4-meal structure, 3x carbs vs. Grind)
- **Science:** Slowly reintroducing carbs/fats while monitoring weight prevents overshoot
- **Benefit:** Metabolism fully recovers; body weight stabilizes at new set point
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 7: Maintenance phase mechanics)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (maintenance_protocol)

## 6. Living in Balance Framework

### Four Pillars of Sustainable Health
1. **Consistent Physical Activity:** "Engaging in consistent and enjoyable physical activity with some resistance training if possible"
2. **Healthy Diet Foundation:** "Eating a predominantly healthy diet as outlined in sections 1-5"
3. **Flexible Meals:** "Having a few relaxed meals here and there" (junk food allowed, not tracked)
4. **Weight Monitoring:** "Checking your body weight every two weeks or so"

**Adaptive Rules:**
- Weight slightly high → eat only healthy foods + reduce carbs/fats for 1 week
- Weight right or low → continue current plan
- No mandatory tracking of relaxed meals
- Don't need to monitor weight obsessively if clothes fit and feel good

**Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 8: "Living in Balance")
**Maps to:** ngx_philosophy_expanded.md (sustainable_lifestyle)

### Anti-Pattern: Rigid Body Weight Monitoring
- **Correction:** "Don't have to monitor body weight at all if you don't want to"
- **Alternative:** Clothes fit, appearance, energy level, and performance metrics suffice
- **Source:** Healthy Eating Made Simple (Section 8, Living in Balance)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (weight_monitoring_flexibility)

## 7. Practical Implementation Signals

### "Grind" Mode Exit Criteria (Switch to "Chill")
Any ONE of these for 2+ consecutive days:
1. Excessive hunger (subjective "crazy hunger")
2. Energy/cognitive fatigue (can't work, think, or exercise well)
3. Sleep disruption (wake early, can't fall back asleep due to hunger)
4. Lifestyle event (birthday, vacation, personal preference for break)

**Source:** Simple Science Diet (How-To, Step 1)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (adherence_monitoring)

### "Chill" Mode Exit Criteria (Return to "Grind")
All of these for 2+ consecutive days:
1. Significantly less hunger
2. Much more energy vs. Grind
3. Normal sleep restored

**Source:** Simple Science Diet (How-To, Step 2)
**Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (readiness_assessment)

### Long-Term Cycling Protocol
- **Cycle Duration:** Up to 12 weeks continuous Grind/Chill alternation
- **Post-Cycle:** Take minimum 8 weeks of Chill-only (maintenance)
- **Weight Stability:** Keep tabs on weight; allow 1 "fun meal" (junk food replacement) per week during Chill if desired
- **Re-entry:** Can repeat Grind/Chill cycle or continue Chill indefinitely
- **Source:** Simple Science Diet (How-To, Step 3)
- **Maps to:** nutrition_protocols.md (periodized_dieting_model)
