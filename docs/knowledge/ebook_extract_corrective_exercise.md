## 1. MOVEMENT ASSESSMENT PROTOCOLS

### 1.1 Overhead Squat Assessment (OHS)

**Purpose:** Screen for global movement dysfunction, identify muscle imbalances, compensation patterns

**Key Data:**
- **Assessment Position:** Feet shoulder-width apart, arms extended overhead, weight in heels
- **Movement Depth:** Client squats to comfortable depth (observe form, not max depth)
- **Observation Planes:** Frontal (anterior view), sagittal (side view), transverse (rotational)

**Common Faults Identified:**
- **Forward Knee Valgus:** Knees cave inward (indicates weak glutes, tight hip flexors)
- **Forward Torso Lean:** Excessive forward trunk flexion (tight ankle dorsiflexors, weak glutes, hyperextended lumbar spine)
- **Heel Lift:** Heels elevate off ground (ankle dorsiflexion limitation, tight gastrocnemius)
- **Arms Fall Forward:** Loss of shoulder flexion/stability (tight pectoralis, weak scapular stabilizers)
- **Asymmetry:** One-sided movement patterns (muscle imbalance, neuromotor dysfunction)

**Protocol:**
- Step 1: Observe during 5 repetitions without external load
- Step 2: Assess range of motion and compensation patterns
- Step 3: Document primary and secondary dysfunctions
- Step 4: Re-assess after corrective intervention to measure progress

### 1.2 Single-Joint Movement Analysis

**Form Assessments (5-Step Protocol):**

**Step 1: One-Arm Shoulder Press — Form 155**
- **Assessment Focus:** Shoulder stability, scapular control, thoracic mobility
- **Observation:** Scapular winging, shoulder hiking, trunk rotation
- **Key Dysfunction:** Upper crossed syndrome, rotator cuff weakness

**Step 2: One-Arm Shoulder Press — Form 127**
- **Progression:** Increased instability (half-kneeling position)
- **Observation:** Core anti-rotation stability, hip stability

**Step 3: One-Arm Shoulder Press — Form 129**
- **Progression:** Standing unilateral load
- **Observation:** Single-leg stability, contralateral core engagement

**Step 4: One-Arm Shoulder Press — Form 131**
- **Progression:** Dynamic shoulder mobility with load
- **Observation:** Glenohumeral joint stability across planes

**Step 5: One-Arm Shoulder Press — Form 132**
- **Progression:** Complex, multi-planar movement
- **Key Focus:** Integration of corrected movement patterns

### 1.3 Postural Assessment (Static)

**Key Data:**
- **Frontal Plane Assessment:** Assess head position, shoulder height, hip alignment, knee valgus/varus, ankle inversion/eversion
- **Sagittal Plane Assessment:** Observe forward head posture, thoracic kyphosis, lumbar lordosis, knee extension/flexion, ankle dorsiflexion
- **Transverse Plane Assessment:** Evaluate shoulder rotation, thoracic rotation, pelvic rotation

**Client Positioning:**
- Double Leg Stance (standing posture)
- Smartphone Assessment Option (compare posture to reference image via computer/smartphone, page 213)

**Postural Stability Hold Assessment (page 182):**
- **Protocol:** Hold static posture against external perturbation or resistance
- **Key Data:** Ability to maintain neutral alignment under challenge
- **Maps to:** assessment_protocols.md, periodization_progression.md

---

### 1.4 Gait Analysis

**Key Data:**
- **Frontal Plane Gait:** Observe for hip drop (single leg stance phase), knee valgus, foot inversion/eversion
- **Sagittal Plane Gait:** Assess stride length, heel strike pattern, hip extension/flexion, knee extension
- **Transverse Plane Gait:** Monitor hip internal/external rotation, trunk rotation, pelvic rotation

**Common Gait Dysfunctions:**
- **Pronation Distortion Pattern:** Excessive foot pronation during stance, knee valgus, internal hip rotation
- **Supination Pattern:** Feet supinate excessively, ankles invert, external hip rotation
- **Gluteal Amnesia Gait:** Weak hip extensors, hamstring dominance, shortened stride

**Walking Assessment:**
- Observe 20-30 meters of natural gait
- Document compensation patterns
- Assess symmetry between left/right sides

## 2. COMMON MOVEMENT DYSFUNCTIONS & MUSCLE IMBALANCES

### 2.1 Upper Crossed Syndrome (UCS)

**Definition:** Muscle imbalance pattern affecting shoulders, neck, and upper back (page 213)

**Overactive (Tight) Muscles:**
- Pectoralis major and minor
- Upper trapezius
- Sternocleidomastoid (SCM)
- Levator scapulae

**Underactive (Weak) Muscles:**
- Lower trapezius
- Serratus anterior
- Deep neck flexors (longus colli, longus capitis)
- Rhomboids

**Postural Presentation:**
- Forward head posture
- Rounded shoulders
- Scapular winging
- Forward shoulder position
- Exaggerated thoracic kyphosis

**Associated Pain:**
- Neck pain
- Shoulder impingement
- Upper back tension
- Headaches

**Assessment Methods:**
- Postural assessment with forward head measurement
- Pectoralis major/minor tightness tests
- Scapular positioning analysis
- Upper trapezius length assessment

**Source:** Chapter on Postural Assessment, page 213

**Maps to:** assessment_protocols.md, exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

---

### 2.2 Lower Crossed Syndrome (LCS)

**Definition:** Muscle imbalance pattern affecting lower back, hips, and pelvis (page 226)

**Overactive (Tight) Muscles:**
- Erector spinae (low back extensors)
- Hip flexors (iliopsoas, rectus femoris)
- Tensor fasciae latae (TFL)
- Adductors
- Gastrocnemius

**Underactive (Weak) Muscles:**
- Gluteus maximus and medius
- Rectus abdominis (deep core)
- Transverse abdominis
- Tibialis anterior

**Postural Presentation:**
- Anterior pelvic tilt (excessive lumbar lordosis)
- Forward head posture
- Hyperextended knees
- Weak glutes, prominent buttocks
- Excessive lumbar curve

**Associated Pain:**
- Low back pain
- Hip flexor tightness
- Knee pain
- Ankle impairment

**Assessment Methods:**
- Postural assessment (standing, side view)
- Thomas Test (hip flexor tightness)
- Supine knee-to-chest (glute and hamstring length)
- Single-leg stance assessment (gluteal strength)
- Pelvic alignment observation

**Source:** Chapter on Lower Body Correctives, page 226

**Maps to:** assessment_protocols.md, exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

---

### 2.3 Pronation Distortion Pattern (PDP)

**Definition:** Movement dysfunction characterized by excessive foot pronation, knee valgus, internal hip rotation (page 164)

**Muscle Imbalances:**
- **Overactive:** Tibialis posterior, hip adductors, TFL, hip internal rotators
- **Underactive:** Tibialis anterior, gluteus medius, vastus medialis obliquus (VMO)

**Postural Presentation:**
- Feet point inward (toed-in stance)
- Knee valgus (knees cave inward)
- Hip internal rotation dominance
- Arch collapse (flat feet appearance)
- Genu valgum appearance

**Movement Faults:**
- Excessive foot pronation in squat/lunge
- Knee valgus during loaded movements
- Single-leg stance with pelvic drop on opposite side

**Assessment Methods:**
- Overhead squat assessment (primary screen)
- Single-leg stance observation
- Foot arch assessment (wet test or visual)
- Hip internal/external rotation ROM test
- Navicular drop test

**Source:** Chapter on Lower Body Correctives, page 164

**Maps to:** assessment_protocols.md, exercise_database.md

---

### 2.4 Flat Back (Hypolordosis)

**Definition:** Loss of natural lumbar curve, excessive posterior pelvic tilt (page 191)

**Muscle Imbalances:**
- **Overactive:** Rectus abdominis, hip flexors (eccentric control)
- **Underactive:** Erector spinae (low back extensors), quadratus lumborum

**Postural Presentation:**
- Minimal lumbar lordosis (flat appearance)
- Posterior pelvic tilt
- Flexed knees (slight)
- Rounded thoracic spine
- Posterior weight shift

**Assessment Methods:**
- Postural assessment (side view, lumbar curve observation)
- Backward bending assessment (limited extension ROM)
- Prone extension test
- Quadratus lumborum length assessment

**Source:** Chapter on Spinal Correctives, page 191

**Maps to:** assessment_protocols.md, exercise_database.md

---

### 2.5 Excessive Lordosis (Hyperlordosis)

**Definition:** Excessive lumbar curve, anterior pelvic tilt (page 195)

**Muscle Imbalances:**
- **Overactive:** Erector spinae, hip flexors, quadratus lumborum
- **Underactive:** Rectus abdominis (deep core), transverse abdominis, glutes

**Postural Presentation:**
- Excessive lumbar curve
- Anterior pelvic tilt
- Hip flexor tightness (legs pulled forward)
- Weak core appearance
- Weight shifted forward through knees

**Associated Pain:**
- Low back pain during extension
- Hip flexor tightness and pain
- Possible anterior knee pain

**Assessment Methods:**
- Postural assessment (side view, lumbar curve prominence)
- Forward bending assessment (limited flexion ROM)
- Thomas Test (hip flexor tightness)
- Supine knee-to-chest test

**Source:** Chapter on Lower Body Correctives, page 195

**Maps to:** assessment_protocols.md, exercise_database.md

---

### 2.6 Kyphosis (Thoracic)

**Definition:** Excessive thoracic spinal curve, forward posture (page 3)

**Muscle Imbalances:**
- **Overactive:** Pectoralis major/minor, serratus anterior (over-facilitation), upper trapezius
- **Underactive:** Lower trapezius, rhomboids, middle trapezius, erector spinae (thoracic)

**Postural Presentation:**
- Rounded upper back
- Forward head posture
- Rounded shoulders
- Scapular winging possible
- Chest sunken inward

**Associated Pain:**
- Upper back tension
- Shoulder impingement
- Neck pain
- Thoracic mobility restriction

**Assessment Methods:**
- Postural assessment (side view, thoracic curve observation)
- Thoracic extension ROM test (prone press-up, page 223)
- Pectoralis tightness assessment
- Scapular positioning analysis

**Source:** Chapter on Postural Assessment and Spinal Correctives, page 3

## 3. CORRECTIVE EXERCISE STRATEGY FRAMEWORK: ILAI PROTOCOL

**Overview:** ILAI is the foundational corrective exercise programming model

### 3.1 Phase 1: INHIBIT (Self-Myofascial Release)

**Objective:** Reduce overactivity, decrease muscle tension, restore normal resting length

**Key Data:**
- **Duration:** 30-60 seconds per muscle
- **Pressure:** Moderate (client can tolerate on 5-6/10 pain scale)
- **Frequency:** Daily or 5-6x/week
- **Tools:** Foam roller, lacrosse ball, massage stick

**Muscle Groups to Inhibit (by dysfunction):**

**Upper Crossed Syndrome:**
- Pectoralis major and minor (foam roller, lacrosse ball)
- Upper trapezius (lacrosse ball, massage stick)
- Levator scapulae (lacrosse ball, trigger point)
- Sternocleidomastoid (manual pressure)

**Lower Crossed Syndrome:**
- Erector spinae (foam roller, T-spine mobilization, page 222-223)
- Hip flexors/iliopsoas (foam roller, lacrosse ball on hip crease)
- Tensor fasciae latae and IT band (foam roller, page 231)
- Adductors (foam roller, lacrosse ball)
- Piriformis/glutes (lacrosse ball, SMR)
- Gastrocnemius (foam roller, massage stick, page 235)

**Specific SMR Protocols:**
- **T-Spine Foam Roll (page 222):** Transverse process SMR, 1-2 minutes
- **Plantar Fascia Ball Roll (page 234):** 60 seconds, slow controlled movements
- **Calf Ball Roll (page 234):** Lacrosse ball, 60 seconds each leg
- **Hip Flexor Contract-Relax Stretch (page 230):** SMR + PNF stretch, 2-3 minutes
- **Hamstring Mobility (page 232, locked long/locked short):** SMR with contract-relax, 2 minutes
- **Hamstring Contract-Relax Stretches (page 233):** Proprioceptive neuromuscular facilitation

**Maps to:** exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

---

### 3.2 Phase 2: LENGTHEN (Static & Dynamic Stretching)

**Objective:** Restore normal muscle length, increase ROM, reduce tightness

**Key Data:**
- **Duration:** 20-30 seconds per muscle (static)
- **Repetitions:** 2-3 sets
- **Frequency:** Daily
- **Type:** Static stretches, contract-relax (PNF), dynamic stretching

**Stretching Protocols by Dysfunction:**

**Upper Crossed Syndrome Stretches:**
- **Upper Trapezius and Levator Scapulae Stretches (page 218):** 30 seconds, 2-3 reps
- **Pectoralis Ball Roll (page 221):** Doorway pec stretch, 30-60 seconds each side

**Lower Crossed Syndrome Stretches:**
- **Hip Flexor Contract-Relax Stretch (page 230):** 2-3 contractions, 30 sec holds
- **Thomas Test — Modified (page 227):** Contract-relax stretching protocol
- **Hamstring Contract-Relax Stretches (page 233):** Supine, 2-3 minute protocol
- **Calf Stretch (page 235):** Wall ankle mobilization, 30-60 seconds

**Dynamic Stretches (for warm-up):**
- **Walkouts with Hip Hinge (page 206):** 5-8 reps, dynamic mobility
- **Sphinx with Reach (page 206):** Thoracic mobility + hip extension

**Maps to:** exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

---

### 3.3 Phase 3: ACTIVATE (Isolated Muscle Strengthening)

**Objective:** Restore neuromuscular control, strengthen underactive muscles, rebuild motor patterns

**Key Data:**
- **Duration:** 3-4 weeks per phase
- **Intensity:** Light to moderate (50-70% 1RM)
- **Volume:** 2-3 sets x 8-12 reps per exercise
- **Frequency:** 2-3x/week for corrective work
- **Progression:** Increase reps, then load, then complexity

**Activation Exercises by Dysfunction:**

**Upper Crossed Syndrome Activation:**
- **Lower Trapezius Activation:** Prone Y-raise, prone I-raise, face pulls
  - Protocol: 2-3 sets x 10-15 reps, 2-3x/week
- **Serratus Anterior Activation:** Wall push-up with hip hinge, Y-raise on Swiss ball (page 205)
  - Protocol: 2-3 sets x 10-15 reps, daily or 5-6x/week
- **Deep Neck Flexor Activation:** Chin tuck (page 216), isolation without resistance
  - Protocol: 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps, daily
- **Rhomboid Activation:** Prone horizontal abduction, wall elbow extension
  - Protocol: 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps, 2-3x/week

**Lower Crossed Syndrome Activation:**
- **Gluteus Maximus Activation:**
  - Single-leg hip thrust (page 231): 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps per leg
  - Quadruped hip extension: 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps per leg
- **Gluteus Medius Activation:**
  - Lateral band walk (mini band, page 196): 2-3 sets x 15-20 steps
  - Side-lying hip abduction: 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps
- **Deep Core/Transverse Abdominis Activation:**
  - Dead bug: 2-3 sets x 8-10 reps per side
  - Bird dog: 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps per side
  - Standing pallof press: 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps per side
- **Tibialis Anterior Activation:**
  - Seated ankle dorsiflexion: 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps
  - Standing marching with dorsiflexion: 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps

**Activation Phase Characteristics:**
- **Movement Pattern:** Single-joint, isolated movements
- **Environment:** Stable, minimal balance challenge
- **Feedback:** High sensory input, cueing for proper activation
- **Intensity:** Low to moderate
- **Progression:** Increase reps first, then load

**Maps to:** exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

---

### 3.4 Phase 4: INTEGRATE (Multi-Joint Movement Patterns)

**Objective:** Restore functional movement, combine corrected patterns into compound movements, prepare for performance training

**Key Data:**
- **Duration:** 3-4 weeks+ (ongoing)
- **Intensity:** Moderate to high (70-85% 1RM)
- **Volume:** 2-3 sets x 6-10 reps per exercise
- **Frequency:** 2-4x/week for strength/power development
- **Progression:** Increase load, decrease stability, increase complexity

**Integration Exercises by Dysfunction:**

**Upper Crossed Syndrome Integration:**
- **Overhead Press Progressions (page 155-132):** 5-step protocol
  - Step 1: One-arm shoulder press (form 155)
  - Step 2: Half-kneeling one-arm press (form 127)
  - Step 3: Standing one-arm press (form 129)
  - Step 4: One-arm press with step (form 131)
  - Step 5: One-arm press with dynamic movement (form 132)
  - Protocol: 3 sets x 6-10 reps, progress weekly
- **Ball Wall Push with Hip Hinge (page 224):** 3 sets x 8-12 reps
- **Posterior Shoulder Ball Roll (page 225):** Combined stretch + activation

**Lower Crossed Syndrome Integration:**
- **Goblet Squat Progression (page 171-172):**
  - Form 171: Goblet squat (basic form)
  - Form 172: Lower body squat (loaded progression)
  - Protocol: 3 sets x 8-10 reps, increase load weekly
- **Reverse Goblet Lunge with Band (page 193):** 3 sets x 8-10 reps per leg
- **Hip Hinge Pattern with Mini Band (page 196):** 3 sets x 8-10 reps
- **Restore Alignment and Stability Protocol (page 174-181):**
  - Step 1 (page 175): Hip hinge + band activation
  - Step 2 (page 178): Single-leg stance + core challenge
  - Step 3 (page 178): Loaded squat position
  - Step 4 (page 180): Trunk stability roll
  - Step 5 (page 181): Hip stability + movement integration

**Pronation Distortion Pattern Integration:**
- **Lateral Step with Mini Band (page 198):** 3 sets x 15-20 steps per direction
- **Monster Walk (page 199):** Quad + glute activation, 3 sets x 15-20 steps
- **Squat with Mini Band (page 194):** Goblet squat variation, glute engagement cue
- **Single-leg exercises:** Single-leg deadlift, split squat, step-up (emphasizing glute medius control)

**Spinal Correctives Integration:**
- **DNS T-Spine Rotation (page 223):** Prone t-spine rotation, 3 sets x 8-10 reps per side
- **Hard Style Plank (page 191):** 3 sets x 30-45 second holds
- **Trunk Stability Roll (page 180):** 3 sets x 8-10 reps per direction
- **Postural Stability Hold with Movement (page 201):** Head movements added to static posture
- **Monster Walk with Upper Body Rotation:** Integrated movement pattern

**Maps to:** exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

## 4. JOINT-SPECIFIC CORRECTIVE PROTOCOLS

### 4.1 SHOULDER CORRECTIVE PROTOCOL

**Assessment:**
- **Overhead squat assessment** (observe arm position, range of motion)
- **Pectoralis major/minor length tests** (tightness assessment)
- **Scapular positioning analysis** (wing, protraction, upward/downward rotation)
- **Rotator cuff strength testing** (empty can test, external rotation strength)
- **Glenohumeral mobility and stability** (page 141): Mobility vs. stability paradigm

**Key Dysfunctions:**
- Upper crossed syndrome (primary)
- Rotator cuff insufficiency
- Scapular dyskinesis (winging, asymmetry)

**Corrective Progression:**

**Phase 1 — INHIBIT (1-2 weeks):**
- Pectoralis myofascial release (foam roller, lacrosse ball): 60 sec, daily
- Upper trapezius/levator scapulae release: 60 sec, daily
- Thoracic spine SMR (T-spine foam roll, page 222): 1-2 min, daily

**Phase 2 — LENGTHEN (2-3 weeks):**
- Doorway pectoralis stretch: 30 sec x 2-3, daily
- Upper trapezius/levator scapulae stretch (page 218): 30 sec x 2-3, daily
- Thoracic extension ROM (prone press-up, page 223): 8-10 reps x 2

**Phase 3 — ACTIVATE (3-4 weeks):**
- Lower trapezius activation (prone I/Y-raise): 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps, 3x/week
- Serratus anterior activation (wall push-up with hip hinge, page 205): 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps, daily
- Rotator cuff activation (external rotation with band): 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps, 3x/week
- Deep neck flexor activation (chin tuck, page 216): 2-3 sets x 10 reps, daily

**Phase 4 — INTEGRATE (4+ weeks):**
- One-arm shoulder press progressions (page 155-132): 5-step protocol, 3 sets x 8-10 reps
- Posterior shoulder ball roll + activation (page 225): 3 sets x 8-10 reps
- Ball wall push with hip hinge (page 224): 3 sets x 8-12 reps

**Return-to-Performance Criteria:**
- Full overhead ROM without compensation
- Scapular stability (no winging, symmetric positioning)
- Shoulder stability in loaded positions (75%+ strength vs. bilateral)
- Pain-free overhead pressing at 85%+ of baseline strength

**Source:** Chapter on Upper Body Correctives, pages 213-225

**Maps to:** exercise_database.md, periodization_progression.md

### 4.2 HIP CORRECTIVE PROTOCOL

**Assessment:**
- **Overhead squat assessment** (knee valgus, pelvic tilt observation)
- **Single-leg stance** (pelvic drop, hip stability)
- **Thomas Test** (hip flexor tightness, page 227)
- **Supine knee-to-chest test** (glute/hamstring length)
- **Hip internal/external rotation ROM** (glenohumeral joint mobility, page 141)
- **Gait analysis** (foot position, knee tracking, pelvic alignment)

**Key Dysfunctions:**
- Lower crossed syndrome (primary)
- Pronation distortion pattern
- Gluteal amnesia (weak glutes)
- Hip flexor tightness with anterior pelvic tilt

**Corrective Progression:**

**Phase 1 — INHIBIT (1-2 weeks):**
- Hip flexor SMR (foam roller, lacrosse ball on hip crease): 90 sec, daily
- Tensor fasciae latae/IT band release (foam roller, page 231): 90 sec per leg, daily
- Piriformis/glutes release (lacrosse ball): 60 sec per side, daily
- Adductor SMR (foam roller, lacrosse ball): 60 sec, daily
- Gastrocnemius/soleus release (foam roller, page 235): 90 sec per leg, daily

**Phase 2 — LENGTHEN (2-3 weeks):**
- Hip flexor contract-relax stretch (page 230): 2-3 contractions x 30 sec, daily
- Hamstring contract-relax stretch (page 233): 2-3 contractions x 30 sec, daily
- Calf stretch (wall ankle mobilization, page 235): 30 sec x 2-3, daily
- Thomas Test — Modified stretch (page 227): 30 sec x 2-3, 5-6x/week

**Phase 3 — ACTIVATE (3-4 weeks):**
- Gluteus maximus activation:
  - Single-leg hip thrust (page 231): 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps per leg, 3x/week
  - Quadruped hip extension: 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps per leg, 3x/week
- Gluteus medius activation:
  - Lateral band walk with mini band (page 196): 2-3 sets x 15-20 steps, 3x/week
  - Side-lying hip abduction: 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps per leg, 3x/week
- Tibialis anterior activation:
  - Seated ankle dorsiflexion: 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps, daily
  - Standing marching with dorsiflexion: 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps, 3x/week

**Phase 4 — INTEGRATE (4+ weeks):**
- Goblet squat progression (page 171-172): 3 sets x 8-10 reps, 3x/week
- Lateral step with mini band (page 198): 3 sets x 15-20 steps per direction, 3x/week
- Monster walk (page 199): 3 sets x 15-20 steps, 3x/week
- Hip hinge pattern with glute activation: 3 sets x 8-10 reps, 3x/week
- Restore alignment and stability protocol (page 174-181): 5-step progression, 2-3x/week
- Single-leg exercises (single-leg deadlift, split squat, step-up): 3 sets x 6-8 reps

**Return-to-Performance Criteria:**
- Full hip flexion/extension ROM without compensation
- Gluteal activation on single-leg stance (no pelvic drop)
- Knee alignment in squat (no valgus)
- Equal strength bilaterally (glutes, hip flexors, abductors)
- Pain-free loaded hip exercises at 85%+ of baseline strength

**Source:** Chapter on Lower Body Correctives, pages 164-206, 226-235

### 4.3 KNEE CORRECTIVE PROTOCOL

**Assessment:**
- **Overhead squat assessment** (knee valgus, varus, forward tracking)
- **Single-leg stance** (knee stability, pelvic control)
- **Knee flexion/extension ROM** (joint mobility)
- **Knee strategy in squat** (page 164): Knee valgus vs. varus observation
- **Gait analysis** (foot position, ankle pronation, pelvic control)

**Key Dysfunctions:**
- Pronation distortion pattern (primary cause of knee valgus)
- Weak glutes (leads to internal hip rotation, knee valgus)
- Ankle dorsiflexion limitation (forward knee translation, patellar stress)
- Tibialis anterior weakness

**Corrective Progression:**

**Phase 1 — INHIBIT (1-2 weeks):**
- Plantar fascia ball roll (page 234): 60 sec, daily
- Calf release (foam roller, page 235): 90 sec per leg, daily
- Quadriceps release (foam roller): 90 sec per leg, 5-6x/week
- Tensor fasciae latae/IT band release (foam roller, page 231): 90 sec per leg, daily

**Phase 2 — LENGTHEN (2-3 weeks):**
- Calf stretch (wall ankle mobilization, page 235): 30 sec x 2-3, daily
- Ankle dorsiflexion ROM work (active dorsiflexion): 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps, daily
- Quadriceps stretch: 30 sec x 2-3, 5-6x/week

**Phase 3 — ACTIVATE (3-4 weeks):**
- Tibialis anterior activation (seated dorsiflexion): 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps, daily
- Gluteus medius activation (lateral band walk, page 196): 2-3 sets x 15-20 steps, 3x/week
- Vastus medialis obliquus (VMO) activation (short arc quads with band): 2-3 sets x 12-15 reps, 3x/week
- Gluteus maximus activation (single-leg hip thrust, page 231): 2-3 sets x 10-12 reps, 3x/week

**Phase 4 — INTEGRATE (4+ weeks):**
- Goblet squat (page 171-172): 3 sets x 8-10 reps, 3x/week (with glute medius cue)
- Lateral step with mini band (page 198): 3 sets x 15-20 steps, 3x/week
- Monster walk (page 199): 3 sets x 15-20 steps, 3x/week
- Single-leg squat progressions: 3 sets x 6-8 reps per leg
- Single-leg deadlift (with glute emphasis): 3 sets x 6-8 reps per leg

**Return-to-Performance Criteria:**
- Knee alignment in squat (no valgus/varus)
- Full knee flexion/extension ROM without pain
- Single-leg stability (no pelvic drop or knee collapse)
- Ankle dorsiflexion sufficient for squat depth
- Pain-free loaded knee exercises at 85%+ of baseline strength

**Source:** Chapter on Lower Body Correctives, pages 164-206

### 4.4 ANKLE/FOOT CORRECTIVE PROTOCOL

**Assessment:**
- **Foot posture** (pronation vs. supination, arch height)
- **Ankle dorsiflexion/plantarflexion ROM** (mobility assessment)
- **Heel strike pattern in gait** (foot position, pronation timing)
- **Single-leg stance** (ankle stability, proprioception)
- **Overhead squat assessment** (ankle dorsiflexion requirement visible)

**Key Dysfunctions:**
- Excessive foot pronation (flat feet, arch collapse)
- Limited ankle dorsiflexion (plantarflexor tightness)
- Tibialis anterior weakness
- Ankle proprioceptive deficit
- Plantar fasciitis (plantarfascia tightness)

**Corrective Progression:**

**Phase 1 — INHIBIT (1-2 weeks):**
- Plantar fascia ball roll (page 234): 60-90 sec daily
- Calf release (foam roller, lacrosse ball, massage stick, page 235): 90 sec per leg, daily
- Gastrocnemius/soleus SMR: 90 sec per leg, daily
- Foot intrinsic muscle release (golf ball on arch): 60 sec daily

**Phase 2 — LENGTHEN (2-3 weeks):**
- Calf stretch (wall ankle mobilization, page 235): 30-60 sec x 2-3, daily
- Plantarflexor PNF stretch (contract-relax): 2-3 contractions, daily
- Dorsiflexion ROM work (active ROM): 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps, daily
- Foot intrinsic activation (short foot exercise): 2-3 sets x 10-15 reps, daily

**Phase 3 — ACTIVATE (3-4 weeks):**
- Tibialis anterior activation (seated dorsiflexion): 2-3 sets x 15-20 reps, daily
- Foot intrinsic activation (toe yoga, short foot): 2-3 sets x 10-15 reps daily

## 7. RETURN-TO-PERFORMANCE CRITERIA

### Progressive Return Framework

**Phase 1: Corrective (Weeks 1-4)**
- Objective: Restore muscle balance, reduce compensation patterns
- Goals: ILAI phases 1-2 complete (inhibit + lengthen)
- Assessment: Movement faults reduced by 50%+
- Criteria: Pain-free SMR and stretching, improved ROM
- Duration: 2-4 weeks (typically shorter for fitness populations)

**Phase 2: Activation (Weeks 3-6)**
- Objective: Restore neuromuscular control, rebuild strength
- Goals: ILAI phase 3 (activate) complete
- Assessment: Isolated exercises pain-free, strength gains measurable
- Criteria:
  - Single-joint exercises at 75%+ of bilateral strength
  - Activation patterns visible (EMG feedback or palpation)
  - Pain-free ROM in all planes
  - No compensation patterns during isolation
- Duration: 2-4 weeks

**Phase 3: Integration (Weeks 5-12)**
- Objective: Restore functional movement patterns
- Goals: ILAI phase 4 (integrate) progression
- Assessment: Multi-joint movements match pre-injury baseline
- Criteria:
  - Compound movements at 85%+ of bilateral strength
  - Single-leg exercises equal to bilateral baseline
  - Movement quality matches non-injured side
  - No pain during loaded activities
  - Full ROM throughout all planes
- Duration: 4-8 weeks

**Phase 4: Return-to-Performance (Weeks 12+)**
- Objective: Full strength, power, and sport/training return
- Goals: Sport-specific or training-specific movements
- Assessment: Advanced testing (Max rep test, movement complexity tests)
- Criteria:
  - Strength equal to or exceeding pre-injury baseline (100%+)
  - Power production (vertical jump, broad jump, single-leg hop) ≥90% of baseline
  - Agility and cutting movements without compensation
  - Sport/training-specific exercises at full intensity
  - Psychological readiness confirmed

---

### Return-to-Performance Criteria by Dysfunction

**Upper Crossed Syndrome:**
- Forward head posture corrected (ears aligned over shoulders)
- Scapular positioning symmetric (no winging, proper retraction)
- Shoulder flexion to 180° without arm falloff
- Overhead pressing at 90%+ of baseline strength
- Pain-free rotator cuff exercises at resistance level

**Lower Crossed Syndrome:**
- Neutral pelvic tilt in standing (no anterior tilt)
- Hip flexors lengthened (able to stand with pelvic neutral)
- Glute strength equal bilaterally (single-leg test)
- Squat to 90° hip/knee flexion without compensation
- Loaded deadlift at 90%+ of baseline strength

**Pronation Distortion Pattern:**
- Foot arch maintained in loaded movements (no excessive pronation)
- Knee alignment in squat/lunge (no valgus)
- Single-leg stance 45+ seconds without pelvic drop
- Glute medius strength equal bilaterally
- Lateral movements (side step, lateral bound) without knee valgus

**Ankle/Foot Dysfunction:**
- Ankle dorsiflexion ROM 15+ degrees (sufficient for squat depth)
- Calf/plantarfascia mobility symmetrical
- Single-leg balance 60+ seconds on firm surface, 30+ on unstable surface
- Single-leg hop distance ≥90% of non-injured leg
- Gait pattern symmetric, heel strike timing normal

**Spinal Dysfunction (Low Back):**
- Neutral lumbar curve in standing posture
- Lumbar flexion/extension ROM within normal limits
- Core endurance plank hold 60+ seconds
- Deadlift at 90%+ of baseline strength with neutral spine
- No pain during loaded spinal movements

## 10. KEY STATISTICS & REFERENCE DATA

### Normal Ranges & Benchmarks (from textbook assessment sections)

**Ankle Dorsiflexion ROM:**
- Normal: 15-20 degrees (sufficient for squat depth)
- Limited: <15 degrees (indicates gastrocnemius/soleus tightness, risk factor for knee pain)

**Hip Flexion ROM:**
- Normal: 120+ degrees (Thomas Test supine)
- Limited: <120 degrees (indicates hip flexor tightness, anterior pelvic tilt risk)

**Shoulder Flexion ROM:**
- Normal: 180 degrees overhead (no compensation)
- Limited: <160 degrees (indicates mobility restriction or postural dysfunction)

**Core Plank Hold Duration:**
- Beginner: 20-30 seconds
- Intermediate: 30-60 seconds
- Advanced: 60+ seconds

**Single-Leg Stance Duration:**
- Baseline: 30 seconds (eyes open, firm surface)
- Goal: 60+ seconds (eyes open, firm surface)
- Advanced: 30+ seconds (unstable surface)

**Lower Extremity Functional Scale (LEFS):**
- Score Range: 0-80
- <20: Severe dysfunction (high disability)
- 20-40: Moderate dysfunction
- 40-60: Mild dysfunction
- 60+ : Near normal function
- Normal: 78-80 (no disability)

**Upper Extremity Functional Index (UEFI):**
- Score Range: 0-100
- <50: Severe dysfunction
- 50-75: Moderate dysfunction
- 75+ : Mild/near normal

**Corrective Phase Duration:**
- Fitness Population: 2-4 weeks (shorter due to baseline strength)
- General Population: 4-8 weeks (longer due to varied baseline)
- Return-to-Sport Population: 8-12 weeks (complete proprioceptive restoration)

## 11. CLINICAL NOTES & SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### Red Flags Requiring Medical Referral

- Sharp, radiating pain (possible nerve involvement)
- Numbness/tingling (neurological deficit)
- Pain that increases during corrective exercises (not DOMS)
- Asymmetric strength loss >25% between limbs
- Inability to achieve neutral spine position after 4 weeks corrective work
- Balance dysfunction (possible vestibular issue)

### Modifications by Population

**Sedentary/Desk Worker:**
- Prioritize: Upper crossed syndrome correction, hip flexor lengthening
- Extended inhibit phase (3-4 weeks SMR)
- Frequent stretching (throughout day, 5-6x/week)
- Slower progression to integration

**Athlete/Fitness Population:**
- Prioritize: Activation + integration (faster progression)
- Shorter inhibit phase (1-2 weeks)
- Rapid progression to compound movements
- Earlier return-to-sport timeline

**Older Adult (60+):**
- Extended assessment phase (detailed ROM testing)
- Slower load progression
- Balance training integrated early
- Falls prevention focus
- Modified exercise variations (seated, assisted)
